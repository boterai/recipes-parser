About the Recipe & Method
The secret to a perfect Upma recipe lies in roasting the rava until it releases a nutty aroma, which prevents the grains from becoming lumpy once cooked. Once the rava is roasted, a quick tempering of mustard seeds, urad dal, curry leaves and green chillies builds the base flavour, followed by sautéing onions to add sweetness and texture. After adding hot water and salt, you let the rava absorb the liquid on low flame while stirring occasionally. A splash of lemon juice and a pinch of sugar balances the flavours beautifully before garnishing with fresh coriander.

 

Tips & Serving Suggestions
One of the best things about breakfast upma is how adaptable it is — feel free to boost nutrition by mixing in diced carrots, peas, beans, or even potatoes during cooking. It’s also easy to tweak texture by adjusting the water-to-semolina ratio depending on whether you prefer soft and moist or light and fluffy upma. Serve your upma hot with coconut chutney, sambhar, or a steaming cup of chai for a complete South Indian breakfast experience that’s sure to impress your family.

Ingredients

For Upma

  • 1 cup semolina (rava / sooji)
  • 1 1/2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds ( rai / sarson)
  • 1 tsp urad dal (split black lentils)
  • 4 curry leaves (kadi patta)
  • 2 slit green chilli
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • salt to taste
  • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • 2 tsp sugar

For The Upma Garnish

  • 1 tbsp finely chopped coriander (dhania)

Method

For upma
 

  1. To make upma, heat the semolina in a kadhai and dry roast on a mdium flame for 4 to 5 minutes, while stirring occasionally. Keep aside.
  2. Heat the oil in a kadhai and add the mustard seeds.
  3. When the seeds crackle, add the urad dal, curry leaves and green chillies and sauté on a medium flame for a few seconds.
  4. Add the onions and sauté on a medium flame for 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Add the roasted semolina, 3 cups of hot water and salt and mix well and cover with a lid and cook on a slow flame for 3 to 4 minutes, while stirring occasionally.
  6. Add the lemon juice and sugar, mix well and cook on a medium flame for another 1 minute, while stirring continuously.
  7. Fill the upma in a glass bowl and demould on a plate.
  8. Serve the upma immediately garnished with coriander.

How to make Upma

 

    1. To make upma, heat a kadhai and add the 1 cup semolina (rava / sooji) into it.

    2. Dry roast on a medium flame for about 4 to 5 minutes, while stirring occasionally. Switch off the flame when the semolina turns slightly pink in colour.

    3. Transfer the roasted rava to a plate and keep it aside. You will get a beautiful aroma when the rava is being roasted to perfection. You can roast a large quantity of rava , store it in a jar and use it as and when you wish to make Upma.

    4. Heat the 1 1/2 tbsp oil in another kadhai.

    5. Add the 1/2 tsp mustard seeds ( rai / sarson). It is an essential part of most South Indian Recipes.

    6. When the seeds begin to splutter, add the 1 tsp urad dal (split black lentils) into the kadhai.

    7. Next, add the 4 curry leaves (kadi patta).

    8. Add the 2 slit green chilli for the spice. You can also use dry red chilies and finely chopped ginger to enhance the flavour of the Upma further.Sauté this tempering on a medium flame for a few seconds. Do NOT burn the tempering.

    9. Add the 1/4 cup finely chopped onion and sauté on a medium flame for 1 to 2 minutes. Onions provide the desired crunch and flavour to the Upma.

    10. Now, its time to add the roasted semolina (rava / sooji) into the kadhai.

    11. Add the salt to taste.

    12. Also add 3 cups of hot water to cook the semolina.

    13. Mix all the ingredients well and cover with a lid. Cook on a slow flame for about 3 to 4 minutes, while stirring occasionally.

    14. Open the lid and add the 2 tsp lemon juice for the desired subtle tartness.

    15. Also add the 2 tsp sugar to balance the flavours.

    16. Mix the well and cook on a medium flame for not more than 1 minute, while stirring continuously. Cooking for a long period of time, after the addition of lemon can make it bitter.

    17. Add 1 tbsp finely chopped coriander (dhania).

    18. Transfer the hot  Upma (Quick Upma Recipe) into a greased glass bowl to give it the shape of the bowl.

    19. Demould Upma on a plate by tapping the bowl lightly.

    20. Serve the upma immediately garnished with coriander.

FAQs

1. What is Upma?
Upma is a popular Indian breakfast dish made mainly with semolina (also called rava or sooji). It’s cooked with spices, onion and water to a soft, fluffy texture.

2. What ingredients do I need to make this Upma?
Basic ingredients include semolina, oil, mustard seeds, urad dal, curry leaves, green chillies, onion, salt, lemon juice, sugar and coriander for garnish.

3. How long does it take to prepare and cook?
This recipe takes about 5 minutes of preparation plus 12 minutes of cooking, so the total time is around 17 minutes.

4. Can I make Upma healthier?
Yes  you can add boiled vegetables such as carrots, beans, peas, potatoes or tomatoes along with semolina to increase fiber and nutrition.

5. Do I need to roast the semolina?
Yes  the semolina is dry-roasted first on a medium flame for about 4–5 minutes to enhance flavor and prevent lumping.

6. What’s the water to semolina ratio?
The recipe calls for about 3 cups of hot water for 1 cup of semolina to cook it properly.

7. Why do we add lemon and sugar?
Lemon juice adds a subtle tartness and sugar balances flavors. Stir thoroughly but don’t over-cook after adding lemon, or it can become bitter.

8. Can I make variations of Upma?
Yes  besides this rava upma, you can try oats upma or other versions like vermicelli upma or vegetable upma for variety.

9. How should I serve Upma?
It is best served hot — often in an inverted bowl shape and garnished with fresh coriander. Chutney or sambhar can be served on the side.

10. How many servings does this recipe make?
This Quick Upma recipe makes about 4 servings, perfect for a family breakfast.

Tips for Upma Recipe

1. Roast the Rava Well

Dry roast the semolina (rava/sooji) until it turns slightly golden and aromatic. This prevents lumps and gives a richer flavour.

2. Use Hot Water

Add hot water when cooking the roasted rava. This helps it absorb liquid faster and avoids clumping.

3. Balance Water Ratio

Stick to about 3 cups of water for 1 cup of rava. Too much water makes it mushy; too little makes it dry.

4. Customize with Veggies

Boost nutrition and taste by adding veggies like carrots, peas, beans, or potatoes during cooking.

5. Add Lemon at the Right Time

Add lemon juice after cooking the upma and mix quickly. Adding it too early can make the dish slightly bitter.

6. Ghee for Extra Aroma

Finish with a spoon of ghee at the end (optional). It enhances flavour and gives a silky texture.

7. Stir Continuously After Lemon

Stirring continuously for about a minute after adding lemon helps distribute flavours and keeps the upma light and fluffy.

8. Use a Thick-Bottomed Pan

A thick-bottomed kadhai or pan distributes heat evenly, reducing chances of burning the seeds or dal during tempering.

9. Add Tempering Extras

Consider adding urad dal, chana dal, cashews, or ginger in the tempering for more texture and taste.

10. Garnish Freshly

Garnish with fresh coriander just before serving and serve hot with chutney or a squeeze of extra lemon.
