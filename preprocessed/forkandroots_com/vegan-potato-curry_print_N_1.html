Vegan Potato Curry

  • Author: Tereza Flores

Description

Rich, creamy, and warming—this plant-based potato curry proves that simple ingredients can create deeply satisfying comfort food that’s ready in under 40 minutes.

Prep Time: 10 minutes | Cook Time: 30 minutes | Total Time: 40 minutes | Servings: 4

Ingredients

Scale
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced into 1-inch cubes (Yukon Gold or russets work beautifully)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (fresh is crucial!)
  • 1 can (14 oz) full-fat coconut milk (canned, not carton—essential for creaminess!)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth (low-sodium recommended)
  • 1 tbsp curry powder (your flavor powerhouse)
  • 1 tsp turmeric (naturally vegan and adds gorgeous golden color)
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil for sautéing (coconut or vegetable oil work perfectly)
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (essential for brightness)
  • Cooked rice, for serving (basmati or jasmine are perfect)

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add your diced onion and minced garlic, stirring occasionally until they’re translucent and smell absolutely incredible—about 5-6 minutes. Don’t rush this flavor-building step; this is where the magic starts!
  2. Add your diced potatoes to the pot and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. This gives them a head start and helps them absorb flavors better. Every ingredient has its own personality, and potatoes benefit from this initial cooking.
  3. Here’s my plant-based secret: add your curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and paprika directly to the pot with the potatoes. Stir everything together for about a minute, toasting those spices with the potatoes and aromatics. This blooms their flavors and creates incredible aromatic depth. The kitchen will smell like pure comfort food heaven.
  4. Pour in your coconut milk and vegetable broth, stirring to combine everything beautifully. Season generously with salt and pepper—plant-based cooking needs bold flavors to really shine. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer.
  5. Reduce the heat to low and let your curry simmer gently for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are fork-tender and the sauce has thickened slightly. Don’t worry if you’re new to plant-based cooking—just let it bubble away gently and the potatoes will become perfectly creamy while absorbing all those gorgeous spices.
  6. Taste and adjust your seasonings. I usually add more salt or a squeeze of lime juice for brightness. Trust your palate!
  7. Serve your gorgeous curry over fluffy cooked rice and garnish with fresh cilantro if you can resist diving in immediately. Every spoonful should have that perfect balance of creamy sauce, tender potatoes, and aromatic spices!

Notes:

  • Don’t skip toasting the spices with the potatoes—this 1-minute step creates incredible depth of flavor that makes this taste like it’s been simmering for hours.
  • Use full-fat canned coconut milk, not the carton stuff. The canned version creates that rich, creamy sauce this curry needs.
  • This tastes even better the next day after the potatoes have absorbed all those flavors. Make it ahead for easy weeknight dinners.
  • The curry will thicken as it sits because potatoes absorb liquid. Just add a splash of coconut milk or broth when reheating.

Storage Tips:

  • Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days. Add liquid when reheating since it thickens considerably.
  • Freeze in individual portions for up to 3 months for easy grab-and-go meals. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stovetop.
  • The coconut milk may separate when frozen, but a quick stir brings it right back together. Plant-based curries freeze beautifully!

Serving Suggestions:

  • Serve over fluffy basmati rice, brown rice, or quinoa for a complete meal
  • Pair with warm naan bread or roti for dipping and scooping
  • Top with a dollop of coconut yogurt and extra cilantro for cooling contrast
  • Add a side of mango chutney or lime pickle for authentic Indian accompaniment

Mix It Up (Vegan Recipe Variations):

Spinach Power Curry: Stir in 2 cups fresh spinach during the last 3 minutes for extra nutrients and gorgeous green color that wilts down perfectly.

Aloo Gobi Style: Add 1 cup frozen peas and 1 cup cauliflower florets with the potatoes for classic Indian vegetable curry vibes.

Tomato-Based Red Curry: Add 1 can diced tomatoes with the liquids for tangier, more complex flavor that tastes like restaurant-style curry.

Sweet Potato Version: Use half regular potatoes and half sweet potatoes for natural sweetness and beautiful orange color—nutritional powerhouse variation.

What Makes This Plant-Based Recipe Special:

This curry uses simple, affordable ingredients—just potatoes, coconut milk, and pantry spices—but creates restaurant-quality depth through proper technique. Toasting the spices before adding liquid blooms their essential oils, while simmering allows the potatoes to absorb flavors and naturally thicken the sauce. This proves that satisfying plant-based comfort food doesn’t require expensive ingredients or complicated methods—just smart cooking techniques that coax maximum flavor from humble, wholesome ingredients.
