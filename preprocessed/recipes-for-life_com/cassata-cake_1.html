Cassata Cake Recipe | Classic Italian-American Dessert

Laura Hauser

November 22, 2025

Once a traditional Sicilian dessert, cassata cake has become a beloved Italian-American celebration cake. Our version maintains the classic flavors while streamlining the process for home bakers. This recipe delivers the authentic taste with modern techniques.

Why This Recipe Works

The sponge cake base provides a sturdy foundation that absorbs the liqueur syrup without becoming soggy, ensuring perfect texture throughout the assembly process and maintaining structural integrity when sliced

    CLASSIC APPLE DAPPLE CAKE Fall Baking Dessert

    Using high-quality ricotta cheese and proper draining techniques creates a rich, creamy filling that holds its shape while delivering authentic flavor without the graininess that can occur with inferior preparation methods

    The combination of citrus zest and high-quality chocolate in the filling provides balanced flavor complexity that complements rather than overwhelms the delicate sponge cake and sweet frosting elements

    Chilling the assembled cake for the recommended time allows flavors to meld properly and makes clean slicing possible, resulting in professional-looking portions that maintain their layered structure

    The simplified frosting technique using stabilized whipped cream provides the traditional appearance without requiring advanced decorating skills, making this impressive dessert accessible to bakers of all experience levels

    Ingredients

    • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • 1 cups granulated sugar
    • 4 large eggs at room temperature
    • cup unsalted butter softened
    • 1 cup whole milk
    • 2 teaspoons baking powder
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • teaspoon salt
    • 32 ounces whole milk ricotta cheese drained
    • 1 cups powdered sugar
    • 1 cup mini chocolate chips
    • cup orange zest
    • 2 tablespoons lemon zest
    • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
    • cup orange liqueur such as Grand Marnier
    • cup simple syrup
    • 2 cups heavy whipping cream
    • cup powdered sugar for frosting
    • cup candied fruit for decoration
    • 2 tablespoons chopped pistachios

    Equipment Needed

    • Two 9-inch round cake pans
    • Stand mixer or electric hand mixer
    • Mixing bowls various sizes
    • Fine mesh strainer
    • Parchment paper
    • Cake turntable optional
    • Offset spatula
    • Serrated knife
    • Cooling rack
    • Pastry brush

    Instructions

    Prepare the Sponge Cake Layers

    Preheat your oven to 350F. Prepare two 9-inch round cake pans by greasing them thoroughly and lining the bottoms with parchment paper circles. In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, combine the softened butter and granulated sugar. Beat on medium speed for 3-4 minutes until the mixture becomes light and fluffy, scraping down the sides of the bowl periodically to ensure even mixing.

    Add the eggs one at a time, waiting until each egg is fully incorporated before adding the next. This gradual addition prevents the batter from breaking and ensures proper emulsification. In a separate bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt until well combined. With the mixer on low speed, alternate adding the dry ingredients and whole milk to the butter mixture, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients.

    Mix just until combined after each addition, being careful not to overmix which can develop gluten and create a tough cake texture. Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and bake for 25-30 minutes until the cakes spring back when lightly pressed and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Cool the cakes in their pans for 10 minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.

    Make the Ricotta Filling

    Place the ricotta cheese in a fine mesh strainer set over a bowl and allow it to drain for at least 30 minutes to remove excess moisture. This step is crucial for achieving the proper filling consistency that wont make the cake layers soggy. In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained ricotta with 1 cups powdered sugar, orange zest, lemon zest, and cinnamon. Use a rubber spatula to fold these ingredients together until well combined, being careful not to overmix which could make the filling grainy.

    Gently fold in the mini chocolate chips until evenly distributed throughout the mixture. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the filling to firm up slightly. This chilling period makes the filling easier to spread and helps prevent it from squishing out when you assemble the cake layers. The filling should be thick but spreadable if it seems too stiff after chilling, let it sit at room temperature for 10 minutes before using.

    Assemble the Cake Layers

    Use a long serrated knife to carefully slice each cooled cake layer horizontally into two even layers, creating four thin cake layers total. Place one cake layer on your serving plate or cake stand and use a pastry brush to generously apply a mixture of orange liqueur and simple syrup to the surface. The syrup should moisten the cake without making it soggy youll use approximately 2-3 tablespoons per layer.

    Spread one-third of the chilled ricotta filling evenly over the moistened cake layer, going all the way to the edges but leaving a slight border to prevent spillage. Repeat this process with the remaining cake layers, syrup mixture, and filling, ending with a plain cake layer on top. Gently press down on the assembled layers to ensure theyre evenly stacked and the filling is distributed consistently. Refrigerate the assembled cake for at least 1 hour before frosting to allow the structure to set and make the frosting process easier.

    Prepare the Whipped Cream Frosting

    Chill your mixing bowl and whisk attachment in the freezer for 15 minutes before beginning. Pour the heavy whipping cream into the chilled bowl and begin whipping on medium speed until soft peaks form, which typically takes 2-3 minutes. Gradually add the cup powdered sugar while continuing to whip, increasing the speed to medium-high once the sugar is incorporated. Continue whipping until stiff peaks form, being careful not to over-whip which can cause the cream to become grainy and eventually turn to butter.

    The frosting should hold its shape when the whisk is lifted but still remain smooth and spreadable. If the kitchen is warm, you can stabilize the whipped cream by adding 1 tablespoon of instant pudding mix during the whipping process, though this is optional. Use the frosting immediately as whipped cream can begin to separate if left standing too long.

    Frost and Decorate the Cake

    Remove the chilled cake from the refrigerator and place it on a cake turntable if available. Apply a thin layer of whipped cream frosting over the entire cake to create a crumb coat, which seals in any loose crumbs and provides a smooth base for the final frosting layer. Chill the cake for 15 minutes to set the crumb coat. Apply the remaining whipped cream frosting to the chilled cake, using an offset spatula to create smooth sides and an even top.

    For decorative piping, reserve about cup of frosting in a piping bag fitted with a star tip. Create decorative borders around the top and bottom edges of the cake if desired. Arrange the candied fruit and chopped pistachios in an attractive pattern on top of the cake. Refrigerate the completed cake for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, before serving to allow the flavors to fully develop and the structure to set properly for clean slicing.

    Tips and Tricks

    For optimal cake texture, ensure all refrigerated ingredients like eggs and milk reach room temperature before beginning. Cold ingredients dont incorporate as well and can result in a denser cake. When measuring flour, use the spoon-and-level method rather than scooping directly from the container to avoid packing too much flour into your measurements.

    This small detail can significantly impact the final cake texture. If your ricotta seems particularly wet, you can drain it for up to 2 hours or even overnight in the refrigerator wrapped in cheesecloth. Some bakers prefer to weight the ricotta with a plate to expedite the draining process. When splitting cake layers, use a serrated knife with a gentle sawing motion and rotate the cake as you cut to maintain an even thickness.

    You can also use toothpicks as guides by inserting them at the halfway point around the cakes circumference. For the liqueur syrup, you can adjust the ratio to suit your taste preferences some prefer more liqueur for stronger flavor while others prefer more simple syrup for sweetness. If serving to children or avoiding alcohol, you can substitute the liqueur with orange juice mixed with a teaspoon of orange extract. When applying the syrup to the cake layers, work quickly but thoroughly to ensure even distribution without oversaturating any one area.

    The cake should feel moist but not wet to the touch. For the whipped cream frosting, work in a cool environment and consider placing a bowl of ice water beneath your mixing bowl if your kitchen is warm. This helps the cream whip faster and hold its structure better.

    If you need to transport the cake, chill it thoroughly first and consider using a cooler with ice packs to maintain temperature. When slicing the finished cake, use a sharp knife dipped in hot water and wiped clean between cuts for perfectly clean slices that showcase the beautiful layers.

    Recipe Variations

    Chocolate Cassata: Replace one cup of all-purpose flour with cocoa powder in the cake batter and add cup of espresso to enhance the chocolate flavor. For the filling, use dark chocolate chunks instead of mini chips and consider adding a tablespoon of instant espresso powder to complement the chocolate notes. The whipped cream frosting can be replaced with chocolate ganache or chocolate buttercream for a richer dessert option that still maintains the layered structure characteristic of cassata cake.

    Lemon Berry Cassata: Substitute the orange liqueur with limoncello in the soaking syrup and add cup of lemon juice to the ricotta filling instead of orange zest. Fold 1 cup of fresh raspberries or sliced strawberries into the filling along with the chocolate chips. Decorate the top with fresh berries and mint leaves instead of candied fruit for a brighter, fruit-forward version that works particularly well for spring and summer occasions.

    Nut-Free Version: Omit the pistachio garnish and replace with toasted coconut flakes or additional candied fruit. You can also add teaspoon of almond extract to the cake batter to provide nutty flavor without actual nuts. For those with severe allergies, ensure your chocolate chips are produced in a nut-free facility and consider using vegetable-based simple syrup instead of store-bought versions that may contain traces of nuts.

    Individual Portions: Instead of a large layer cake, bake the batter in a sheet pan and use a round cutter to create individual cake rounds. Layer these with the ricotta filling in small glasses or mason jars for single-serving desserts that are perfect for parties. This variation allows for easier serving and portion control while maintaining all the traditional flavors in a modern presentation format.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Can I make cassata cake ahead of time?

    Yes, cassata cake actually benefits from being made ahead. The assembled cake can be refrigerated for up to 2 days before serving, which allows the flavors to meld and the texture to set perfectly.

    If making further in advance, you can prepare the components separately: bake and freeze the cake layers for up to 3 months, make the ricotta filling and refrigerate for 2-3 days, and prepare the whipped cream frosting on the day of assembly. The completed cake freezes well for up to 1 month if wrapped properly in plastic wrap and aluminum foil, though the whipped cream texture may change slightly upon thawing.

    What can I substitute for orange liqueur?

    If you prefer not to use alcohol, you can create a non-alcoholic syrup using equal parts orange juice and simple syrup with 1 teaspoon of orange extract added for intensified flavor. For other alcoholic alternatives, rum or brandy work well with the citrus and chocolate notes. Amaretto provides an interesting almond flavor that complements the pistachio garnish. When substituting, maintain the same liquid measurements and adjust sweetness by tasting the syrup mixture before applying to the cake layers.

    Why is my ricotta filling watery?

    Watery ricotta filling typically results from insufficient draining of the cheese or overmixing that breaks down the cheese structure. To fix this, drain the ricotta longer up to 4 hours in a cheesecloth-lined strainer in the refrigerator. If the filling is already prepared and too wet, you can add 2-3 tablespoons of instant vanilla pudding mix to help thicken it without affecting flavor. Another option is to fold in cup of crushed ladyfinger cookies or cake crumbs to absorb excess moisture while adding texture.

    How do I prevent the whipped cream from deflating?

    Proper temperature is crucial for stable whipped cream. Ensure your cream, bowl, and beaters are thoroughly chilled before beginning. Whip at medium speed rather than high to incorporate less air and create a denser, more stable foam. Adding 1 tablespoon of instant pudding mix or 1 teaspoon of gelatin dissolved in 1 tablespoon of warm water can significantly improve stability. Avoid overwhipping, which causes the fat molecules to separate and the cream to become grainy. Apply the frosting to a thoroughly chilled cake and keep the completed cake refrigerated until serving.

    Can I use different cake types for cassata?

    While sponge cake is traditional, other cake types can work with adjustments. Pound cake provides a denser, richer base that holds up well to the filling but may require less soaking syrup. Angel food cake creates a lighter texture but needs careful handling during assembly. For a time-saving option, high-quality store-bought sponge cake or ladyfingers can be used, though the texture and absorption qualities will differ. The key is choosing a cake that can absorb moisture without becoming mushy while providing structural support for the heavy ricotta filling.

    Summary

    This cassata cake combines moist sponge layers with rich ricotta filling and light whipped cream frosting. The method ensures proper texture and flavor balance while maintaining traditional elements. Chilling time is essential for perfect slicing and flavor development.
