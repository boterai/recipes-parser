Polish Pumpkin Soup Recipe (Zupa z Dyni)

5 Stars 4 Stars 3 Stars 2 Stars 1 Star

No reviews

  • Author: Laura
  • Total Time: 35 minutes
  • Yield: 4-6 servings 1x

Description

Polish Pumpkin Soup (Zupa z Dyni) is a creamy and flavorful autumn dish made with fresh pumpkin, warm spices, and topped with homemade grzanki (croutons) and cream. Perfect for a cozy dinner!

Ingredients

Units Scale

For the Soup:

  • 1 medium sugar pumpkin (about 4 lbs), peeled and cubed
  • 4 cups vegetable stock (or chicken broth)
  • 1/2 cup light cream (or half-and-half)
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 tbsp white wine vinegar (or malt vinegar)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

For the Grzanki (Croutons):

  • 4 slices sourdough bread, cubed
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Pumpkin: Cut the pumpkin into cubes and cook in a pot with olive oil for 5-7 minutes.
  2. Simmer the Soup: Add stock, spices, brown sugar, and vinegar to the pot. Simmer for 20 minutes until the pumpkin is tender.
  3. Blend the Soup: Purée the soup using an immersion blender or a regular blender until smooth.
  4. Add Cream: Stir in the cream and heat for 2 more minutes.
  5. Prepare the Grzanki: Toss cubed sourdough bread in olive oil and bake for 10-12 minutes until crispy.
  6. Serve: Ladle the soup into bowls and top with grzanki, cream, and fresh parsley.

Notes

For a spicier kick, add a pinch of chili flakes or smoked paprika.

This soup is naturally gluten-free if you omit the grzanki or use gluten-free bread.

The soup can be made with canned pumpkin, but fresh pumpkin provides a better texture and flavor.
