Why I Love This Polish Sourdough Rye Bread

This rustic Sourdough Rye Bread is a staple of my Polish kitchen. There’s something magical about the tradition of sourdough baking that connects me to my heritage with every loaf I make. Perfect for serving with butter or loading with toppings for traditional Polish Open Faced Sandwiches – Kanapki. It’s not just about making bread; it’s about creating something that brings people together, nourishing both body and soul.

How to Store and Enjoy Sourdough Rye Bread

Freshly baked bread is always best, but this Sourdough Rye Bread holds up beautifully in storage. Once cooled, wrap it in a clean kitchen towel and store it in a breadbox or paper bag for a few days. To keep it longer, slice and freeze, then toast to perfection when you’re ready to enjoy a slice.

Expert Tips for Perfecting Your Loaf

  • Starter Savvy: Use a homemade rye sourdough starter for that signature tangy flavor.
  • Hydration Matters: Lukewarm water activates the starter—too hot, and you might kill the yeast; too cold, and it won’t activate properly.
  • Mixing Method: Combine the ingredients gently. You’re nurturing, not battling the dough.
  • The Right Flour: Bread flour type 680 is the perfect match for this recipe, giving your loaf structure and softness.
  • Salt Timing: Add salt after the autolysis process to ensure gluten development is not hindered.

Toppings and Variations

  • Classic Comfort: Simply buttered, it’s a taste of heaven.
  • Sandwich Star: Layer it with cold cuts, cheese, and pickles for a traditional Polish “kanapka”.
  • Modern Twist: Try it with avocado and a poached egg for a trendy brunch option.

Sourdough Rye Starter Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 150 g whole rye flour (type 2000)
  • 150 g lukewarm water (20-30°C)
  • 1-liter jar with a lid

Instructions:

Day 1: Starting Your Sourdough

  1. Prepare a 1-liter jar and its lid.
  2. Add 30 g of whole rye flour and 30 g of lukewarm water to the jar.
  3. Mix the contents thoroughly until well combined.
  4. Place the jar in a shaded spot. The initial mixing should take about 5 minutes.

Day 2: Observation and Feeding

  1. You might notice small bubbles forming in the mixture—this indicates that fermentation is starting.
  2. Add another 30 g of whole rye flour and 30 g of lukewarm water to the jar.
  3. Stir the mixture thoroughly and return it to its shaded spot.

Day 3: Active Fermentation

  1. By now, bubbles should be more evident, and the mixture might have increased in volume.
  2. Add 30 g of whole rye flour and 30 g of water to the jar.
  3. Mix well and set aside in the same spot.

Day 4: Maintenance

  1. The starter may seem less active or even appear inactive, which is normal.
  2. Continue the feeding process by adding 30 g of rye flour and 30 g of water.
  3. Stir the mixture and put it back in its spot.

Day 5: Ready to Bake

  1. Your sourdough rye starter should now be ready to use for baking flavorful homemade bread.
  2. Proceed with your bread recipe, using the starter to create the dough.

Description

Learn traditional Polish baking with this rustic Sourdough Rye Bread. Start the process a day ahead to allow time for the sourdough to develop its characteristic flavor.

Ingredients

Units Scale
  • 200 grams Active rye sourdough starter (look up for instructions)
  • 380 ml Lukewarm water
  • 600 gram Bread flour (type 680 or equivalent)
  • 1 teaspoon Salt

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Sourdough Starter: Combine the active rye sourdough starter, lukewarm water, and bread flour in a bowl. Do not add salt yet.
  2. First Dough Mixture: Mix the ingredients until just combined to form a thick but not stiff dough. Cover with a cloth or plastic wrap and let rest for about 1 hour for autolysis.
  3. Kneading: After resting, add the salt to the dough and knead on a wet surface with wet hands, folding the dough over itself, for 15-20 minutes until the dough is less sticky and holds its shape.
  4. Fermentation: Transfer the dough back into the bowl, cover, and let it rise for 3-5 hours depending on the room temperature.
  5. Shaping the Loaf: After the dough has risen, shape it into a round or oval loaf and place it in a floured basket or bowl. Cover with a cloth or plastic wrap.
  6. Leave Overnight (optional): Optionally, place the shaped dough in the refrigerator overnight for 6-10 hours to develop flavors.
  7. Baking: Preheat the oven to 240°C (460°F). Heat a covered baking dish in the oven. Carefully place the dough into the hot baking dish, score the top, cover, and bake for 30 minutes. Then uncover and continue baking for another 20 minutes or until fully baked.

Notes

• The sourdough starter should be active and fed a few hours before using.

• For a crispier crust, place a tray with water at the bottom of the oven to create steam during the first part of baking.

• Allow the bread to cool completely before slicing.

FAQ

What makes rye sourdough different from regular sourdough?

Rye sourdough has a unique flavor profile—deep, hearty, and slightly sour, which stands out even with simple toppings.

Can I use only rye flour for this bread?

Using only rye can make the bread very dense, so it’s usually combined with bread flour for a lighter texture.

How do I know when my sourdough bread is fully baked?

The loaf should sound hollow when tapped on the bottom, and the internal temperature should reach 190-210°F.
