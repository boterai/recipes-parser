Christmas Tiramisu

Luca Romano
November 11, 2024
No Comments
  • Pinterest
  • Facebook
  • 20shares

Ah, Christmas! A time when the air is filled with the scent of pine and the warmth of family gatherings. What better way to celebrate than with a twist on the classic Italian dessert, tiramisu?

This Christmas Tiramisu is a festive delight, layered with rich mascarpone and a hint of holiday spirit. Each bite is a creamy, dreamy experience that captures the essence of the season.

The secret? A splash of coffee liqueur and a sprinkle of cocoa, bringing together flavors that dance on the palate. It’s the perfect ending to any holiday feast, guaranteed to leave everyone asking for seconds.

So, gather your loved ones, roll up those sleeves, and let’s create a dessert that will make this Christmas unforgettable!

Ingredients

To recreate this delightful Christmas Tiramisu for four people, gather the following ingredients that will bring out the festive flavors and textures.

  • 1 cup strong brewed coffee, cooled
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur, optional
  • 8 ounces mascarpone cheese, at room temperature
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 24 ladyfinger cookies
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • 1 ounce dark chocolate, shaved or grated, for garnish
  • Fresh rosemary sprigs, for garnish
  • Pinch of sea salt

Instructions

Creating a festive Christmas Tiramisu is a wonderful way to celebrate the season with a delicious dessert. Follow these steps to craft this delightful treat using the specified ingredients.

  1. Brew the coffee: Start by brewing 1 cup of strong coffee and allow it to cool completely. This will serve as the base for soaking the ladyfingers, imparting a rich flavor to the dessert.
  2. Mix the coffee liqueur: If desired, add 2 tablespoons of coffee liqueur to the cooled coffee for an extra layer of flavor. Stir well to combine the ingredients.
  3. Prepare the mascarpone mixture: In a mixing bowl, combine 8 ounces of mascarpone cheese with 1 cup of heavy cream, 1/2 cup of granulated sugar, 3 large egg yolks, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and a pinch of sea salt. Whip the mixture until it becomes smooth and creamy.
  4. Soak the ladyfingers: Quickly dip 24 ladyfinger cookies into the coffee mixture, ensuring they are coated but not overly soggy. Arrange them in a single layer at the bottom of your serving dish.
  5. Layer the mascarpone mixture: Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the layer of soaked ladyfingers, smoothing it out evenly with a spatula to create a nice, even layer.
  6. Repeat the layers: Add another layer of dipped ladyfingers on top of the mascarpone layer, followed by the remaining mascarpone mixture. Smooth the top layer for a polished finish.
  7. Chill the tiramisu: Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the dessert to set properly.
  8. Dust with cocoa powder: Before serving, sift 2 tablespoons of unsweetened cocoa powder over the top of the tiramisu for a classic finish that adds a touch of elegance.
  9. Garnish the dessert: Finally, sprinkle shaved or grated dark chocolate on top and add fresh rosemary sprigs for a festive touch that enhances both the presentation and flavor.

Tweaks and Additions

Elevate your festive Christmas Tiramisu with these delightful tweaks and additions that will enhance both flavor and presentation. These suggestions will make your dessert even more special for the holiday season.

  • Add spices: Incorporate a pinch of ground cinnamon or nutmeg into the mascarpone mixture for a warm, holiday-inspired flavor that complements the coffee and chocolate.
  • Use flavored coffee: Brew the coffee using a flavored variety, such as hazelnut or vanilla, to add an extra layer of complexity to the dessert.
  • Include orange zest: Add a teaspoon of freshly grated orange zest to the mascarpone mixture for a bright, citrusy note that balances the richness of the dessert.
  • Experiment with different liqueurs: Instead of coffee liqueur, try using Amaretto or Irish cream for a unique twist that brings a different flavor profile to the tiramisu.
  • Layer with fruit: Incorporate a layer of fresh raspberries or strawberries between the mascarpone layers for a refreshing contrast and a pop of color.
  • Top with whipped cream: Before dusting with cocoa powder, add a layer of lightly whipped cream on top for a fluffy texture that enhances the dessert’s creaminess.
  • Use chocolate shavings: Instead of just grated chocolate, consider using chocolate shavings for a more elegant presentation and a richer chocolate flavor.
  • Serve with a drizzle: Prepare a chocolate or caramel sauce to drizzle over individual servings for added sweetness and a gourmet touch.
