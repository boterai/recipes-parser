Irish Soda Bread

Description

Simple Irish Soda Bread made without yeast. Golden crust, tender inside, and ready in under an hour using pantry staples.

Ingredients

Scale
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons white vinegar
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • ½ teaspoon baking soda

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F and grease a baking sheet.
  2. In a small bowl, mix milk and vinegar. Let sit for 8–10 minutes until slightly curdled.
  3. In a large bowl, stir together flour, sugar, salt, and baking soda.
  4. Pour the milk mixture into the dry ingredients and stir gently until a shaggy dough forms.
  5. Turn dough onto a floured surface and lightly knead just until it comes together.
  6. Shape into a round loaf and place on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Cut a deep X into the top of the dough using a floured knife.
  8. Bake for 30–40 minutes until golden and hollow-sounding when tapped.
  9. Cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Notes

Do not overmix the dough. This bread is best enjoyed the day it’s baked and pairs well with butter, jam, or hearty soups.
